Writing

I have a Bachelor of Arts in English from the University of Calgary (2007) and a Master of Arts in Theory and Criticism from the University of Western Ontario (2011), where I completed a one-hundred and thirty page research project on how meaningful human experience is constructed through the ways in which we use language to develop and individualized model of objects and relationships between them in the world around us. My area of focus is semiology and structure of written and visual communications.

In 2012, I embarked on a long research project through which I considered the meaning and place of Christian belief in today’s advanced, techno-industrial capitalism. In this project, I examined this problem through the popular culture narrative of the Alien series. It was published by Praeger in volume one of God and Popular Culture: A Behind-the-Scenes Look at the Entertainment Industry’s Most Influential Figure in 2015.

I bring this perspective to bear on my writing about technology, where my particular focus is on software and hardware design of emerging technologies. In particular, I am deeply interested in how both hardware and software user interfaces create (and sometimes obscure) meaningful interactions with technology, and determine how they are used, marketed and registered within culture.
